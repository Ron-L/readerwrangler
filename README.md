## Wrangle your reader chaos - Your books, your order

## Extract and Organize Your Online Amazon Kindle Library Easily

ReaderWrangler extracts your complete [online Kindle library from Amazon.com](https://www.amazon.com/yourbooks)‚Äîincluding covers, ratings, descriptions, and reviews‚Äîwith a simple bookmarklet. It then provides a canvas for you to arrange and group them in any order so you can finally organize your collection how you want it!

With hundreds or thousands of books in your collection, great reads get buried and forgotten. ReaderWrangler lets you create custom groups like "Next to Read" or "Time Travel Books," drag and drop books into organized columns, and double-click any cover to see full details‚Äîso you never lose track of what to read next.

## Quick Start

### 1. One-Time Install of Bookmarklet

A. **Visit the [Get Started](https://readerwrangler.com/install-bookmarklet.html) page**

B. **Drag the button there to your bookmarks bar**

### 2. Fetch Your Data from Amazon.com (Initial Setup)

A. **Click the bookmarklet** and select "Go to Library Fetcher Amazon Page"

B. **Click the bookmarklet again** and select "Fetch Library Data"

C. **Click the bookmarklet** and select "Go to Collections Fetcher Amazon Page"

D. **Click the bookmarklet again** and select "Fetch Collections Data"

**üí° Keeping Your Library Current:** Do this once to get your complete library, then repeat occasionally to fetch newly purchased books. ReaderWrangler will merge the new books while preserving your organization.

### 3. Start Organizing!

A. **Click the bookmarklet** and select "Launch App"

B. **Organize your books**: Load the downloaded JSON file into the organizer

**üîí Your Privacy Matters:** All processing happens in your browser - your data never leaves your computer.

## How It Works

**1. Extract Your Online Amazon Kindle Library**

Your online Amazon Kindle library already has your complete collection‚Äîwe just need to extract it. Install our simple bookmarklet, then when you click the bookmarklet, a navigation menu appears. Select "Go to Library Fetcher Amazon Page" to navigate to the correct Amazon page, then click the bookmarklet again and select "Fetch Library Data" to begin extraction‚Äîscraping your book list directly from Amazon's website and saving it to a file on your computer. The bookmarklet grabs all your books with covers, descriptions, ratings, and reviews from Amazon's servers.

‚è±Ô∏è The initial extraction may take a while depending on your library size, but you only need to do it once

üì• Saved to your Downloads folder (or browser's default location)

**2. Organize Your Collection**

Once your library file is saved, launch ReaderWrangler and click the status line that says "No Library" to load it. Instantly see all your books in a visual grid. Now the fun begins‚Äîdrag books into custom columns ("Next to Read," "Favorites," "Series to Binge"), rearrange them however you like, and finally take control of your reading chaos.

**3. Keep It Fresh**

Bought new books on Amazon? Just click the bookmarklet, navigate to the library fetcher page using the menu, then select "Fetch Library Data" again and choose your existing library file when prompted. ReaderWrangler will quickly scrape only the new additions from Amazon‚Äîno need to re-extract your entire collection. Your custom organization stays intact while your library stays current.

**üí° Smart Recovery:** Sometimes Amazon's servers get busy and miss a few book details during the initial extraction. No worries! Every time you refresh your library by clicking the bookmarklet and fetching data again, ReaderWrangler re-attempts to scrape any missing descriptions or reviews from Amazon. Your library gets more complete with each update.
